# SOP-MUN-001: Munin Integration into Multi-Agency Flood Response Workflow

**Document ID:** SOP-MUN-001 v1.0  
**Classification:** OFFICIAL-SENSITIVE  
**Effective Date:** 2026-03-01  
**Review Date:** 2026-09-01  
**Owner:** National Resilience Directorate, Critical Infrastructure Division  
**Applies To:** All Category 1 and Category 2 responders operating within Local Resilience Forums (LRFs) where Munin is deployed

---

## 1. Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the mandatory workflow for integrating the Munin Sovereign Infrastructure Orchestration Platform into multi-agency flood response operations. It defines the roles, triggers, decision gates, and approval sequences that govern how Munin-generated authorisation packets are created, reviewed, and signed during a flood incident that crosses sector boundaries (water, power, telecom).

Munin does not execute actions autonomously. It recommends pre-validated playbooks and generates cryptographically signed authorisation packets. Humans decide and sign; Munin's role is to make the decision defensible and fast.

---

## 2. Scope

This SOP applies to:

- **Incident types:** Flood events, reservoir overflows, pump station failures, and cascading cross-sector failures triggered by water infrastructure incidents.
- **Geography:** Any LRF region where Munin is installed and ingesting SCADA historian data via the protocol translation layer (`engine/protocol_translator.py`).
- **Operational phase:** From initial detection of anomalous telemetry through to packet authorisation and post-incident audit. This SOP does not cover SCADA command execution — Munin v1 is read-only and advisory (`actuatorBoundary.writesToHardware: false`).
- **Regulatory framework:** Civil Contingencies Act 2004 (CCA), Flood and Water Management Act 2010 (FWMA), Environment Agency Operational Standards, and LRF coordination duties.

---

## 3. Roles and Responsibilities

### 3.1 Incident Commander (IC)

- Holds overall command authority for the incident under CCA 2004 Section 2.
- Receives Munin-generated authorisation packets via the Authorisation Console (`/handshakes`).
- Makes the final decision to approve, reject, or request revision of a packet.
- May delegate packet review to a Sector Operator but retains approval authority for packets where `multiSig.threshold >= 3` (critical-consequence actions).

### 3.2 Sector Operator (SO)

- Responsible for a single infrastructure sector (water, power, or telecom).
- Monitors the dependency graph view (`/graph`) for anomalous edges and cascade indicators within their sector.
- Reviews evidence windows presented in the Evidence Panel (`components/EvidencePanel.tsx`) for edges relevant to their sector.
- Provides sector-specific commentary on packet `uncertainty.notes` before escalation.

### 3.3 Ministry Legal Counsel (MLC)

- Reviews the `regulatoryBasis` and `statutoryCompliance` fields of every packet before it reaches authorised status.
- Confirms that the cited statutory references (e.g., CCA-2004, FWMA-2010, EA-OPERATIONAL) are applicable to the current incident scope.
- Validates that the Liability Shield certificate statement (`statutoryCompliance.certificateStatement`, generated by `engine/liability_shield.py`) accurately reflects the legal basis.
- May veto a packet by recording an objection in the audit log (`engine/out/audit.jsonl`).

### 3.4 Munin Operator (MO)

- Trained operator who manages the Munin platform during an incident.
- Runs or monitors the engine pipeline (`engine/run.py`) to ensure graph, evidence, incidents, and packets are current.
- Uses the Munin CLI (`engine/cli.py`) for diagnostic commands:
  - `munin evidence-quality` — verify evidence confidence ratings before packet generation.
  - `munin regulatory UK` — confirm regulatory mapping is loaded for the correct jurisdiction.
  - `munin applicability` — confirm the incident type falls within Munin's validated domain.
- Monitors system health via `/api/health/readiness` and `/api/health/liveness`.
- Ensures the Merkle chain (`lib/merkle.ts`) is intact by running `verifyMerkleChain()` on all new packets.
- Escalates any validation errors from `lib/packet/validate.ts` to the IC.

## 4. Trigger Criteria

A Munin-assisted response is triggered when **any** of the following conditions are met:

### 4.1 Uncertainty Thresholds

| Trigger | Threshold | Source | Action |
|---------|-----------|--------|--------|
| Shadow-link confidence exceeds correlation threshold | `\|correlation\| >= 0.5` AND `stability >= 0.3` | `engine/infer_graph.py` — `infer_edges()` | Edge promoted to active graph; MO notifies IC |
| Packet uncertainty drops below defensible level | `uncertainty.overall < 0.5` | `lib/packet/validate.ts` — line 45 | Packet flagged with warning `"Low confidence — requires additional evidence"` |
| Counterexample windows exceed support windows | `counterexampleCount > supportCount` for any edge | `engine/sensor_health.py` — `build_evidence_windows()` | Edge reliability downgraded; MO investigates sensor health |
| Sensor health alert (missingness, stuck-at, drift) | `missing_ratio > 0.1`, `CV < 0.01`, or `mean_diff > 2 * std_pooled` | `engine/sensor_health.py` | Affected node removed from inference; MO alerts SO |

### 4.2 Shadow-Link Confidence Categories

| Category | Correlation Range | Stability | Recommended Action |
|----------|------------------|-----------|--------------------|
| HIGH | `≥ 0.8` | `≥ 0.7` | Immediate inclusion in cascade simulation |
| MEDIUM | `0.5–0.8` | `0.3–0.7` | Include with elevated uncertainty in packet |
| LOW | `< 0.5` | `< 0.3` | Exclude from packets; log for future analysis |

### 4.3 Incident Severity Categories

| Severity | Criteria | Multi-Sig Requirement | Playbook Example |
|----------|----------|-----------------------|------------------|
| CRITICAL | `>= 15 nodes impacted` OR `open_dam / close_dam` actions | 3-of-4 ministries (`engine/byzantine_resilience.py`) | `power_frequency_instability.yaml` |
| HIGH | `5–14 nodes impacted` OR cross-sector cascade | 2-of-3 signers | `carlisle_flood_gate_coordination.yaml` |
| MODERATE | `1–4 nodes impacted`, single sector | EA Duty Officer single sign-off (`minimum_sign_off: true`) | `flood_event_pump_isolation.yaml` |

## 5. Step-by-Step Procedure: Detection to Packet Approval

### Step 1: Telemetry Ingestion and Normalisation

**Actor:** Automated (Munin engine)

1. SCADA historian data flows through the hardware data diode (`engine/data_diode.py`) into the ingestion layer.
2. `engine/ingest.py` — `ingest_historian_data()` reads CSV streams, parses timestamps, and aligns time-series to a common index.
3. `engine/ingest.py` — `normalize_timeseries()` handles missing values (forward-fill, backward-fill) and outputs `engine/out/normalized_timeseries.csv`.
4. Protocol translation (`engine/protocol_translator.py`) normalises Modbus, DNP3, OPC UA, IEC 61850, Profibus, and BACnet frames to unified format: `timestamp, node_id, value, quality, source_protocol`.

**Validation gate:** MO confirms ingestion status via `/api/ingestion/status`. If data age exceeds 5 minutes, MO raises ingestion alert.

### Step 2: Dependency Graph Inference

**Actor:** Automated (Munin engine)

1. `engine/infer_graph.py` — `build_graph()` constructs the dependency graph.
2. For each node pair, `compute_correlation_with_lag()` tests lag values in `[-300s, +300s]` and selects the lag maximising `|correlation|`.
3. `compute_stability_score()` splits the time-series into 5 overlapping 24-hour windows and calculates `stability = 1 - std(correlations) / mean(|correlations|)`.
4. Edges passing the confidence threshold (`|corr| >= 0.5`, `stability >= 0.3`) are added; top 3 per source node by `|corr| * stability`.
5. Shadow links (cross-sector edges not in any registry) are flagged with `isShadowLink: true`.
6. Output: `engine/out/graph.json`.

**Validation gate:** MO runs `munin evidence-quality` to verify HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW confidence distribution. If > 50% of edges are LOW, MO escalates to IC before proceeding.

### Step 3: Sensor Health Assessment and Evidence Generation

**Actor:** Automated (Munin engine)

1. `engine/sensor_health.py` — `assess_sensor_health()` runs missingness, stuck-at, and drift detection on every node.
2. `build_evidence_windows()` generates evidence windows for every edge: per-window correlation, quality context (missingness, noise, drift), and support type (`support` or `counterexample`).
3. Output: `engine/out/evidence.json`.

**Validation gate:** SO for each sector reviews evidence windows in the Evidence Panel. Nodes with observability score below 0.5 are flagged for physical sensor inspection.

### Step 4: Incident Simulation and Cascade Prediction

**Actor:** Automated (Munin engine)

1. `engine/build_incidents.py` — `build_incidents()` selects incident types (flood, drought, power_instability, chaos_multi_fault, chaos_correlated) and simulates cascades through the graph.
2. `simulate_cascade()` propagates failures along edges using lag values to compute time-to-impact for each node.
3. Output: `engine/out/incidents.json` containing cascade timelines, blast radius, and confidence bands.

**Validation gate:** MO reviews cascade count. If `chaos_correlated` scenarios exceed 20, MO runs `munin applicability` to confirm the incident class is within Munin's validated domain.

### Step 5: Authorisation Packet Generation

**Actor:** Automated (Munin engine), then MO review

1. `engine/packetize.py` — `packetize_incidents()` matches each incident to a playbook YAML in `playbooks/`.
2. `generate_packet()` assembles the packet:
   - `scope.nodeIds` — all impacted nodes from cascade timeline.
   - `regulatoryBasis` — mapped from incident type (e.g., `"Complies with Civil Contingencies Act 2004"`).
   - `evidenceRefs` — top 5 evidence window IDs supporting the action.
   - `uncertainty.overall` — computed as `1.0 - avg(robustness)` of relevant evidence windows.
   - `technicalVerification.simulatedSuccessProb` — base 0.95, adjusted for uncertainty and scope size.
   - `actuatorBoundary.writesToHardware: false` — confirms read-only advisory mode.
3. `determine_multi_sig_requirements()` sets quorum requirements based on playbook risk level and incident scope.
4. `integrate_byzantine_multi_sig_into_packet()` adds Byzantine multi-sig metadata from `engine/byzantine_resilience.py`.
5. `LiabilityShield.enhance_handshake_with_compliance()` adds statutory compliance certificate.
6. `generate_merkle_receipt()` chains the packet to the previous one via SHA-256 hash chain.
7. Each packet is written to `engine/out/packets/{packet_id}.json`.
8. Creation is logged to `engine/out/audit.jsonl` with Merkle receipt hash.

**Validation gate:** MO runs `lib/packet/validate.ts` — `validatePacket()` on each generated packet. Any error-severity issue blocks the packet from advancing to `ready` status. Warning-severity issues (e.g., low confidence, missing evidence refs) are annotated and forwarded to IC.

### Step 6: Operator Review via Authorisation Console

**Actor:** MO, SO, IC

1. MO opens the Authorisation Console at `/handshakes` or via the API at `/api/packets`.
2. For each packet:
   - **Situation Summary:** Read `situationSummary` — confirms incident type and blast radius.
   - **Evidence Review:** Click through `evidenceRefs` to view supporting and counterexample windows in the Evidence Panel. Counterexample windows are highlighted to prevent automation bias.
   - **Uncertainty Display:** Review `uncertainty.overall` displayed as an ordinal label (HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW) alongside the raw probability, plus `uncertainty.notes`.
   - **Diff Panel:** If the packet revises a previous version, use `lib/packet/diff.ts` — `diffPackets()` to view changes in confidence, scope, constraints, and evidence. The `riskDelta` block shows whether confidence improved or degraded.
   - **Multi-Sig Progress Bar:** Shows `multiSig.currentSignatures / multiSig.threshold` — how many signatures are still needed.
3. SO provides sector commentary on any edge they consider unreliable.
4. MLC reviews `regulatoryBasis` and `statutoryCompliance` fields.

### Step 7: Packet Approval and Signing

**Actor:** IC (and co-signers per quorum)

1. IC reviews the complete packet, including SO and MLC annotations.
2. For MODERATE severity (single sign-off): IC clicks "Approve" on the Authorisation Console. The packet `status` advances from `ready` to `authorized`.
3. For HIGH/CRITICAL severity (multi-sig):
   - Each required signer authenticates via the biometric handshake flow (`engine/byzantine_resilience.py` — `BiometricHandshake`): iris verification, palm verification, hardware token, air-gap verification.
   - Each ministry signature is added via `ByzantineResilienceEngine.add_ministry_signature()`.
   - When `multiSig.currentSignatures >= multiSig.threshold`, the packet is authorised.
4. The signing action is recorded via `/api/authorize` (or `/api/decisions/sign`), logged to the Ed25519-signed audit chain (`lib/audit/auditLog.ts`), and the Merkle chain is extended.
5. For v1 (read-only advisory): the authorised packet serves as a documented, defensible recommendation. No SCADA commands are issued.

### Step 8: Post-Incident Audit

**Actor:** MO, MLC

1. MO runs Merkle chain verification: `verifyMerkleChain()` on all packets from the incident.
2. MO runs audit log chain verification: `verifyChainIntegrity()` on `engine/out/audit.jsonl`.
3. MLC reviews all compliance certificates issued during the incident.
4. MO exports the full incident record (packets, evidence, graph, audit log) for archival.
5. If the incident involved shadow-mode comparison, MO generates the shadow report via `engine/shadow_simulation.py` — `generate_shadow_mode_report()`.

## 6. Escalation Procedures

| Condition | Escalation Path |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Packet validation error (severity: `error`) | MO → IC. Packet blocked until error resolved. |
| Packet validation warning (severity: `warning`) | MO annotates; IC decides whether to proceed. |
| Sensor health alert on > 30% of nodes | MO → IC → National Resilience Directorate. Pipeline paused. |
| Merkle chain verification failure | MO → IC → MLC. All packets from broken chain quarantined. No approvals until chain integrity restored. |
| `munin applicability` returns `DO NOT APPLY` | MO → IC. Munin recommendations are suspended; manual coordination resumes per existing LRF procedures. |

## 7. Records and Audit Trail

All records generated under this SOP shall be retained for a minimum of 7 years in accordance with CCA 2004 record-keeping requirements:

- Authorisation packets: `engine/out/packets/*.json`
- Audit log (Merkle-chained): `engine/out/audit.jsonl`
- Evidence windows: `engine/out/evidence.json`
- Dependency graph: `engine/out/graph.json`
- Incident simulations: `engine/out/incidents.json`
- Normalised time-series: `engine/out/normalized_timeseries.csv`
- Shadow-mode reports (if applicable): `engine/out/shadow_replay_results.json`
