# Outreach and Partnerships

Status of institutional engagement. Honest about what's been done, what's planned, and what's realistic.

---

## Current status: early outreach

Munin is pre-revenue, pre-pilot, pre-institutional-validation. The outreach below is the beginning of building credibility with the institutions that would eventually deploy or validate a system like this. Most of these are cold contacts. I'm under no illusion that a prototype gets meetings with sovereign wealth funds — the goal right now is academic partnerships and data access.

---

## Active outreach

### Academic partnerships (highest priority)

**University of Milan** — I'm based in Milan. Exploring partnership for the Horizon Europe infrastructure resilience call (opens May 6, 2026). Milan has relevant departments in environmental engineering and risk analysis. A university partner is required for Horizon Europe applications.

**UK university water engineering groups** — Reaching out to departments with SCADA historian access and flood modelling expertise. The target is a data-sharing agreement for validating shadow-link discovery on real operational data. Universities are the most realistic path to real SCADA data without a commercial contract.

**ETH Zurich** — Exploring as a potential Horizon Europe consortium partner. ETH has strong groups in formal methods and infrastructure security, which align with Munin's TLA+ work and safety case approach.

### Government agencies (public engagement)

**CISA (US)** — The Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency's sector governance team accepts public input on cross-sector risk assessment. Munin's shadow-link discovery directly addresses their mandate under National Security Memorandum 22 for systemic infrastructure risk tools. Contact: sector.governance@cisa.dhs.gov

**Environment Agency (UK)** — Already using their public flood monitoring API (`environment.data.gov.uk`) for the Carlisle demo. The next step is engaging their data team about access to historical SCADA historian exports for validation beyond the public river level data.

**Scottish Government** — Following up on their May 2025 workshop on cross-sector cascading impacts after Storm Eowyn. They're actively studying the exact problem Munin addresses: how cascade failures propagate across infrastructure sectors during extreme weather.

### EU funding

**Horizon Europe — Resilient Infrastructures call** — Opens May 6, 2026. Budget: part of a EUR 650M+ cluster on infrastructure resilience. Requires a European university partner and a consortium of at least 3 organizations. This is the most realistic near-term funding path for a European-based project. Timeline: application due ~Q3 2026, decisions Q1 2027.

## Not pursuing right now (and why)

**Sovereign wealth funds (Mubadala, GIC, Temasek, PIF)** — These invest at Series B+ through warm intros. Cold email from a solo founder with a prototype = deleted. Revisit when there's a pilot deployment and revenue.

**Large defense VCs (Founders Fund, a16z American Dynamism, In-Q-Tel)** — Same issue. These are warm-intro ecosystems. If institutional credibility builds through academic publications and pilot deployments, these become realistic in 12-18 months.

**Corporate VCs (SoftBank, Samsung, Toyota)** — Too early. They want traction metrics and enterprise integration stories, not prototypes.

**Chinese/Russian investment** — Geopolitically incompatible with a sovereign infrastructure security tool. Munin's value proposition is literally "don't trust foreign hardware operators." Taking investment from state-linked funds would undermine the thesis.

---

## What would change this

1. **A working pilot** with a real utility — this opens doors to every institutional conversation above.
2. **A peer-reviewed publication** — academic credibility makes university partnerships and grant applications much easier.
3. **A warm intro** from someone in the defense/infrastructure ecosystem — this is how VCs actually work at this stage.

The priority is #1 and #2. Everything else follows.
