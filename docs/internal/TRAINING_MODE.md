# Training Mode: Infrastructure Crisis Flight Simulator

## 1. Purpose

This document specifies the training mode for the Munin platform. Training mode provides a flight-simulator-style environment where operators practice infrastructure crisis response using scenarios generated by the digital twin (DT-001), without any connection to live infrastructure systems.

---

## 2. Design Rationale

Operator competence is a safety-critical capability. Aviation safety improved dramatically when flight simulators allowed pilots to train for emergencies they had never experienced. Infrastructure operators face the same challenge: real crises are too rare, too consequential, and too varied to learn from experience alone.

Training mode allows operators to:
- Experience realistic cascade failures in a controlled environment
- Practice decision-making under time pressure
- Receive feedback on their performance without real-world consequences
- Build calibrated trust in Munin's recommendations (including learning when to disagree)

---

## 3. Scenario Injection via Digital Twin

### 3.1 Scenario Sources

| Source | Description | Difficulty Range |
|--------|------------|-----------------|
| Historical replay | Reconstruction of documented past incidents with known outcomes | 1-4 |
| Synthetic generation | Algorithmically generated scenarios using the digital twin models | 1-5 |
| Red team authored | Manually designed adversarial scenarios targeting known weaknesses | 3-5 |
| Randomised stress | Stochastic failure injection for Monte Carlo-style training | 2-5 |

### 3.2 Scenario Delivery

Training scenarios are delivered through the same UI as live operations, with the following mandatory differentiators to prevent mode confusion (ref: HAZ-010):

- Full-screen purple border (20px) surrounding all training mode displays
- Persistent "TRAINING MODE" watermark across the primary canvas
- Training mode indicator in all API responses and decision packets
- Physical indicator light (amber) on console hardware where available
- Mode transition requires explicit acknowledgement with typed confirmation phrase

## 4. Operator Decision Scoring

### 4.1 Scoring Dimensions

| Dimension | Weight | Measurement |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Detection speed | 15% | Time from scenario injection to first operator acknowledgement |
| Situation assessment accuracy | 25% | Operator's stated assessment vs. ground truth cascade path |
| Decision quality | 30% | Comparison of operator decisions to pre-computed optimal response |
| Escalation appropriateness | 15% | Were escalations triggered at the right time and level? |
| Communication clarity | 15% | Quality of handover notes and escalation justifications |

### 4.2 Scoring Model

Scores are calibrated to encourage learning rather than competition:

- **0-40%:** Significant development needed; recommended for remedial training
- **40-60%:** Developing competence; areas for improvement identified
- **60-80%:** Competent performance; minor refinements suggested
- **80-95%:** Proficient performance; ready for independent operations
- **95-100%:** Expert performance; considered for scenario authoring and peer mentoring

Scores are visible only to the individual operator and their designated training supervisor. Scores are never used for disciplinary purposes; this policy is documented in the governance framework.

### 4.3 Anti-Gaming Measures

- Scenarios are not repeated identically; parameters vary between sessions
- Scoring algorithm is not disclosed in detail to prevent optimisation against metrics
- "Time to decision" scoring accounts for appropriate deliberation, not just speed
- Operators who always agree with recommendations receive lower scores than those who demonstrate independent critical evaluation

## 5. After-Action Review

### 5.1 Automated Review Generation

After each training session, the system generates an after-action review containing:

1. **Timeline reconstruction:** Side-by-side comparison of scenario events, system recommendations, and operator actions
2. **Decision analysis:** For each decision point, what the operator did vs. what alternatives were available
3. **Cascade accuracy:** How well the operator predicted the cascade trajectory
4. **Scoring breakdown:** Detailed scoring with explanations for each dimension
5. **Improvement recommendations:** Specific, actionable suggestions based on observed patterns

### 5.2 Peer Review

Operators may optionally share their after-action reviews with peers for collaborative learning. Shared reviews are anonymised by default.

### 5.3 Trend Tracking

Individual operators can track their performance over time across:
- Overall score progression
- Performance by scenario type (power, water, telecoms, multi-sector)
- Performance by difficulty level
- Specific dimension improvements (e.g., detection speed, escalation timing)

## 6. Progressive Difficulty Levels

### Level 1: Foundation

- Single-sector incidents with clear causation
- Ample time for decision-making (slow cascade progression)
- Munin recommendations are consistently accurate
- No governance complexity (demo-profile quorum)
- Purpose: Familiarisation with UI and workflow

### Level 2: Developing

- Single-sector incidents with ambiguous initial indicators
- Moderate time pressure
- Munin recommendations are mostly accurate with occasional uncertainties
- Standard governance (pilot-profile quorum)
- Purpose: Building situation assessment skills

### Level 3: Competent

- Multi-sector cascade scenarios
- Realistic time pressure matching documented incident timelines
- Munin recommendations include meaningful uncertainties and occasional errors
- Full governance (production-profile quorum)
- Purpose: Developing cross-sector awareness and governance compliance

### Level 4: Advanced

- Complex multi-sector cascades with secondary failures
- High time pressure with concurrent demands
- Munin recommendations include deliberate errors that operators should catch
- Governance complexity (emergency profile transitions)
- Purpose: Testing independent judgement and stress resilience

### Level 5: Expert

- Adversarial scenarios designed to exploit cognitive biases
- Extreme time pressure with incomplete information
- Munin recommendations may be systematically biased (testing automation bias resistance)
- Governance degradation scenarios (key holder unavailable, communication failure)
- Purpose: Preparing for worst-case operational conditions

## 7. Integration with Certification

Training mode completion at specified levels forms part of the operator certification pathway:

| Certification Level | Training Requirement | Validity |
|---------------------|---------------------|----------|
| Probationary Operator | Level 1-2 completion; minimum 60% score | 3 months |
| Certified Operator | Level 1-3 completion; minimum 70% average | 12 months |
| Senior Operator | Level 1-4 completion; minimum 75% average | 12 months |
| Duty Officer qualified | Level 1-5 completion; minimum 80% average; peer review | 6 months |

All certifications require periodic recertification through additional training sessions.
